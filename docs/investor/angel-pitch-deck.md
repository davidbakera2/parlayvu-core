# ParlayVU Angel Pitch Deck

Draft copy for a 10-12 slide angel/pre-seed deck. Audience: Ann Arbor/Michigan angels first, with language that still works for broader B2B software investors.

## 1. Title

### ParlayVU.ai turns client work into an AI-operated workspace

- Microsoft-native AI operating system for client-facing expert firms.
- Nathan orchestrates specialist AI agents inside Teams, Microsoft 365, and project memory.
- Raising a $100k-$300k angel/pre-seed round to convert working product into repeatable pilots.

Speaker intent: open with practical B2B productivity, not science fiction. ParlayVU is built for firms already living in Microsoft 365.

## 2. Problem

### Client-facing firms lose margin after every call

- Calls, transcripts, notes, assets, tasks, dashboards, emails, and approvals scatter across disconnected tools.
- Generic AI can draft content, but it does not remember the client, manage the workflow, or keep approvals in the operating system.
- Boutique agencies, consultants, coaches, and expert businesses need AI leverage without building an internal AI team.

## 3. Solution

### Nathan runs the client workflow from Teams

- Nathan is the Teams front door and orchestrator for a specialist agent team.
- Agents turn source material into meeting notes, campaign assets, websites, approval packets, email drafts, dashboard inputs, and follow-up.
- Human approvals remain the gate for client-facing sends, publishing, deployments, claims, and commitments.

## 4. Product

### A Microsoft-native AI workspace, not another chatbot

- Teams channels bind to client/project memory, so Nathan answers in context.
- Teams Files publishing creates meeting notes as both `.md` for machine-readable memory and `.docx` for client review.
- Microsoft Graph, agent mailboxes, approval cards, Planner direction, SharePoint Files, and Power BI starter data form the operating surface.
- Dylan can generate Astro/Tailwind campaign sites and route deployment through approvals.

## 5. What Exists Now

### The demo already proves the workflow loop

- FastAPI/LangGraph backend hosted for demo use, with readiness checks for LLM, memory, M365, Teams, avatar, and approval configuration.
- Teams-style Nathan message routing works against project memory.
- RamAir pilot workspace is documented with channel structure, Files folders, meeting notes, approvals, and social performance dashboard starter data.
- M365 Files meeting-note publishing supports `.md` and `.docx` outputs from a client template.
- Tavus/Nathan conversation flow is validated as a provider-hosted avatar session; native Teams audio/video participation remains roadmap/scaffold.

## 6. Why Now

### AI agents are moving into the tools where work already happens

- Microsoft Teams and Microsoft 365 are the default operating surface for many client-facing firms.
- AI has moved from single prompts to agentic workflows, but most SMB teams cannot assemble the plumbing themselves.
- Buyers want productivity gains with control: project memory, approvals, auditability, and client-ready outputs.

## 7. Market Wedge

### Start with firms where expertise becomes client deliverables

- Initial wedge: boutique agencies, consultants, coaches, professional services firms, and expert-led businesses.
- Ideal early customer: Microsoft 365/Teams user with repeatable meetings, content, campaigns, reporting, and approvals.
- Expand from high-touch pilots into repeatable workspace templates for client onboarding, meeting recaps, campaign kits, dashboards, and publishing gates.
- Ann Arbor/Michigan fit: practical B2B software, services-to-product insight, Microsoft-native workflow, and local pilot credibility without limiting the market geographically.

## 8. Business Model

### High-touch pilots now, repeatable workspace subscriptions next

- Early revenue: setup/pilot fee plus monthly subscription.
- Subscription dimensions: active clients/projects, agent seats, integrations, generated outputs, storage, and premium workflow modules.
- Services-assisted onboarding is acceptable early because it exposes repeatable templates and accelerates learning.
- Long-term goal: productized client-workspace packages for agencies and expert firms.

## 9. Go To Market

### Founder-led pilots create the first repeatable playbook

- Run 3-5 RamAir-style pilots with client-facing firms already using Microsoft 365.
- Sell around one concrete workflow: "turn client meetings and source material into approved deliverables and follow-up."
- Use investor and local network introductions to agencies, consultants, Microsoft-centric SMBs, and professional services operators.
- Convert successful pilot patterns into templates: channel setup, meeting notes, approval packets, campaign kits, dashboard starters, and weekly updates.

## 10. Differentiation

### ParlayVU combines project memory, approvals, and outputs inside Microsoft 365

- Not generic chat: Teams-native project memory and client workspace execution.
- Not a content-only tool: agents coordinate notes, sites, drafts, approvals, dashboards, and follow-up.
- Not uncontrolled autonomy: approval gates govern client-facing actions.
- Roadmap advantage: provider-hosted avatar proof plus a conservative native Teams media-bot scaffold for future meeting participation.

## 11. Roadmap And Milestones

### The next 90 days turn the demo into pilot-ready product

- 30 days: polish RamAir pilot workspace, meeting-note flow, approval cards, readiness checks, and demo script.
- 60 days: package onboarding templates for 3-5 pilot firms and tighten M365/Teams Files setup.
- 90 days: convert pilot learnings into repeatable workspace modules, dashboard updates, stronger project-memory ingestion, and early pricing.
- Later: validate native Teams roster participation and audio/video bridge only after Microsoft Graph media, provider terms, and media injection are proven.

## 12. Ask

### Raising $100k-$300k to turn a working product into early revenue

- Use of funds: product hardening, pilot delivery, M365/Teams integration, Tavus/LiveAvatar validation, design/pitch assets, and founder-led go-to-market.
- Milestone by close of round: RamAir pilot polished, 3-5 qualified pilot conversations active, and repeatable workspace templates documented.
- Milestone by 90 days after funding: first paid pilots or signed pilot LOIs, measurable workflow outputs, and clearer subscription packaging.
- Investor asks: small angel checks, pilot introductions, Microsoft 365 operator/customer intros, and Ann Arbor/Michigan angel network referrals.

## Credibility Boundaries

- Claim as working: Teams-style Nathan routing, hosted Azure-oriented backend path, project memory scaffold, M365 Files `.md`/`.docx` meeting notes, approvals, RamAir pilot artifacts, Power BI starter structure, and Tavus provider-hosted Nathan session validation.
- Claim as roadmap/scaffold: autonomous native Teams media bot, real Graph media injection, and Nathan joining Teams meetings as a production audio/video participant.
- Avoid implying mature SaaS traction until paid pilots or signed LOIs exist.
