# ParlayVU One-Page Investor Memo

## Company

ParlayVU.ai is building a Microsoft-native AI operating system for client-facing expert firms. Nathan, the AI orchestrator, works inside Teams and Microsoft 365 to coordinate specialist agents, project memory, client-ready outputs, and human approval gates.

## Problem

Boutique agencies, consultants, coaches, and professional services firms lose margin after every client interaction. Calls, transcripts, source material, tasks, approvals, dashboards, and follow-up scatter across Teams, email, documents, websites, and reporting tools. Generic AI can draft content, but it does not manage the client workflow or keep outputs grounded in project context.

## Solution

ParlayVU gives expert firms an AI team inside their existing Microsoft workflow. Nathan receives requests in Teams, uses client/project memory, routes tasks to specialist agents, and returns meeting notes, campaign assets, websites, approval packets, dashboard inputs, and follow-up. Client-facing sends, publishing, deployments, claims, and commitments remain approval-gated.

## Current Proof

- Working FastAPI/LangGraph backend with Nathan routing and specialist agent registry.
- Project memory scaffold for clients, projects, source assets, generated outputs, approvals, and agent events.
- Teams-style message routing and Teams-ready approval cards.
- Microsoft 365 Files publishing for meeting notes as both `.md` and `.docx`.
- RamAir pilot workspace model with Teams channel structure, Files folders, Planner direction, approval packets, and Power BI starter assets.
- Tavus provider-hosted Nathan conversation validation and HeyGen LiveAvatar scaffolding for grounded project Q&A and approved notes.

Native Teams audio/video avatar participation is not yet production-ready. The Teams media bot and Microsoft Graph media bridge are documented roadmap/scaffold work.

## Market Wedge

The first market is client-facing expert firms already using Microsoft 365: boutique agencies, consultants, coaches, professional services teams, and expert-led businesses. The Ann Arbor/Michigan network is a strong launch path for practical B2B productivity pilots, but the product category is broader than the local market.

## Business Model

Early model: setup or pilot fee plus monthly subscription. Subscription packaging can expand by active clients/projects, agent seats, integrations, generated outputs, and premium workflow modules. Services-assisted onboarding is useful early because it reveals repeatable templates that can become productized workspace packages.

## Go To Market

ParlayVU will run founder-led pilots with 3-5 client-facing firms. The initial pilot promise is concrete: turn client meetings and source material into approved deliverables, updates, and follow-up inside Microsoft 365. Repeatable modules include Teams channel setup, meeting-note publishing, campaign kits, approval packets, Power BI starter dashboards, and weekly updates.
